01

Campaign Sprint · Group Project

Bring Back Bob’s Pizza

An integrated campaign designed to restore a former Evanston favorite and reposition it as the late-night gathering place for Northwestern students.

Challenge

Bob’s Pizza had closed, leaving an opportunity to revive both the brand and a familiar piece of Northwestern’s college-town culture.

Strategy

The campaign used narrative transportation and nostalgia to reach recent Northwestern alumni who valued Bob’s as part of their college experience.

Execution

Recommended tactics included a press release, alumni op-ed, social media copy, fundraising communication, and stakeholder outreach.

My contribution

I helped lay the groundwork for the campaign’s initial strategy and created the visuals that translated the team’s ideas into a cohesive, audience-facing presentation.

Proposed outcome: Reconnect alumni, build public interest, and create support for a viable Bob’s Pizza relaunch.

02

Campaign Sprint · Group Project

A Smarter Hawkins

A fictional mayoral communication campaign focused on guiding Hawkins through crisis with preparation, protection, and transparency.

Challenge

Hawkins faced fear, misinformation, secrecy, and extraordinary public safety threats that demanded credible leadership.

Strategy

The message framed calm, data-informed leadership as the path to community survival and long-term resilience.

Execution

The work included debate preparation, response strategies, memorable message pillars, rebuttals, and a closing statement.

My contribution

I led the data analysis and used the findings to develop strategic recommendations that strengthened the campaign’s positioning and decision-making.

Communication goal: Establish the candidate as a grounded, transparent leader capable of responding decisively during crisis.
